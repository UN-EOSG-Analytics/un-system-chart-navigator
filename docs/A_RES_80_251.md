# A/RES/80/251 — Mandate Creation, Implementation and Review for an Efficient and Effective United Nations

**United Nations General Assembly**
**Eightieth session, Agenda item 123**
**Resolution adopted: 31 March 2026**
**Distributed: 7 April 2026**

---

## Preamble

*The General Assembly,*

- **Reaffirming** the Charter of the United Nations and its purposes and principles,
- **Recalling** its resolutions 79/318 of 18 July 2025 and 80/242 of 30 December 2025 and its decision 79/571 of 2 September 2025,
- **Emphasizing** that the mandate implementation review is one of the essential workstreams to achieve the UN80 Initiative goal of creating a more efficient and effective United Nations system,
- **Recognizing** the importance of other reform, revitalization and rationalization processes under way across the United Nations system,

---

## 1. Guiding Principles (Mandate Life Cycle)

The General Assembly **agrees** that the following principles shall be applied across the United Nations system throughout the mandate life cycle (creation, implementation, and review):

- **(a)** Mandates exist to fulfil the purposes and principles of the UN Charter, delivering maximum impact for Member States and the people served, while accounting for future generations.
- **(b)** The diverse nature of mandates and their contexts, and of membership, governance structures, and financial/administrative arrangements across the UN system, must be respected and preserved.
- **(c)** Decisions on mandates are the exclusive prerogative of Member States.
- **(d)** UN secretariats and stakeholders have an indispensable role in supporting Member States throughout the mandate life cycle.
- **(e)** Efforts to improve mandate creation, implementation, and review shall uphold the balance across the three UN pillars — peace and security, human rights, and development — with no pillar disadvantaged.
- **(f)** A culture of continuous improvement, accountability, enhanced strategic coordination and cooperation, cost-efficiency, and cost-effectiveness is essential for coherent, fit-for-purpose, and timely responses to global challenges.
- **(g)** Full and equal participation of Member States, including through multilingualism and use of official and working languages, must be respected.
- **(h)** Best practices across the UN system shall be embraced throughout the mandate life cycle to maximize impact and efficiency.
- **(i)** Enhanced visibility and transparency of the mandate landscape are required to ensure coherence and avoid unnecessary duplication.
- **(j)** Member States shall exercise discipline and strive for enhanced clarity and brevity when creating mandates.
- **(k)** Member States shall employ evidence-based decision-making throughout the mandate life cycle.
- **(l)** Mandates shall be resourced to ensure effective implementation, in accordance with established programme planning and budgetary processes.
- **(m)** Member States shall fulfil their financial obligations as set out in the Charter — on time, in full, and without conditions.
- **(n)** Mandate implementation shall be regularly reviewed to assess impact and inform decisions on the future of mandates.

## 2. Mandate Creation

### Para. 2 — Concept Notes
Member States **agree** that proponents of new mandates shall provide a concise concept note including:
- Context and rationale
- Objectives and expected impact
- Mandate landscape
- Proposed mandated activities
- Indicative financial implications
- Technical secretariat focal point
- UN system implementation lead
- Implementation timelines

### Para. 3 — Secretariat Support
**Requests** the Secretary-General to make available, upon request, enhanced administrative and technical secretariat support to proponents of mandates, including identification of the technical secretariat focal point, preliminary guidance on a UN system implementation lead, and indicative financial implications.

### Para. 4 — Mandate Registry
**Requests** the Secretary-General to develop the UN Mandate Registry as a critical reference tool by ensuring it is comprehensive, updated regularly, and linked to other mandate source registries across the UN system.

### Para. 5 — Clearer Mandates
Member States **commit** to drafting and adopting clearer and more concise mandates that include:
- Streamlined preambular paragraphs
- Well-defined scopes, objectives, and mandated activities
- Implementation road maps
- Mandate implementation review clauses and timelines
- Mandate retirement clauses (as appropriate)
- Expected funding arrangements

### Para. 6 — Centralized Information for Member States
**Requests** the Secretary-General to make available to Member States, on an ongoing basis and in a centralized location, enhanced information and resources relevant to decision-making throughout the mandate life cycle, including:
- Drafting and process guidance
- Links to established rules and practices
- An indicative cost catalogue of commonly mandated activities
- A list of designated contact points for collaboration with UN system entities

### Para. 7 — Discipline in Mandating
Member States **commit** to exercising discipline by only mandating the convening of meetings or the commissioning of reports or other activities required to achieve the stated objectives of the resolution or decision.

## 3. Mandate Implementation

### Para. 8 — Enhanced Reporting
**Requests** the Secretary-General to enhance reporting on mandate implementation by ensuring reports are:
- Timely, focused, and user-oriented
- Developed using templates with differentiated formats and lengths
- Incorporating user-centred design approaches and greater use of data
- Maintaining transparency and comprehensive coverage of important issues

### Para. 9 — Resource Allocation
Member States **agree** to allocate, in accordance with established programme planning and budgetary processes, the resources necessary for the implementation of mandates with clear budgetary implications that cannot be absorbed within existing resources, and **welcome** efforts to reprioritize existing similar or duplicative activities where possible.

### Para. 10 — Secretary-General Resource Effectiveness
**Encourages** the Secretary-General to enable more effective use of resources through enhanced organizational effectiveness, strengthened budget discipline, and absorption of resource implications within existing resources where appropriate.

### Para. 11 — System-Wide Coordination
**Requests** the Secretary-General (including in capacity as Chair of the UN System Chief Executives Board for Coordination) to facilitate coordination and assignment of implementation responsibilities among Secretariat entities and across the UN system, based on comparative and collaborative advantage, and consistent with decisions of respective intergovernmental mandating organs.

### Para. 12 — Efficiency in Implementation
Member States **agree** that mandates shall be implemented in an efficient and effective manner to ensure maximum impact on the ground.

## 4. Mandate Review

### Para. 13 — Review Clauses
Member States **agree** to include mandate implementation review clauses — which could include clear timelines, indicators, criteria, and mechanisms — in all new and renewing mandates, and **encourage** consideration of review cycles of three to five years.

### Para. 14 — Focus on Outcomes
Member States **commit** to ensuring that mandate implementation reviews focus on outcomes and impact, rather than only activities and outputs.

### Para. 15 — Review Resources
**Encourages** the consistent allocation of resources necessary for conduct of mandate implementation reviews, including by utilizing existing oversight and review mechanisms.

### Para. 16 — Review of Mandated Reports
**Requests** the Secretary-General to review the full portfolio of mandated reports of the Secretary-General to identify candidates for combination or change in periodicity, for presentation to Member States **by the end of June 2026**, for their consideration.

### Para. 17 — Collective and Clustered Reviews
Member States **agree** to explore options to undertake collective mandate implementation reviews across intergovernmental organs and clustered mandate implementation reviews of similar or related mandates.

### Para. 18 — Advice on Review Mechanisms
**Requests** the Secretary-General to provide advice on possible mechanisms to undertake collective and clustered mandate implementation reviews, for presentation to Member States **by the end of September 2026**, for their consideration.

### Para. 19 — Management for Results
**Requests** the Secretary-General to strengthen and harmonize management for results mechanisms across the UN system, including how results are measured and communicated, based on system-wide reporting standards.

### Para. 20 — Mandate Validity
Member States **reaffirm** that, once adopted, mandates remain valid until fully implemented or expired as provided for in their original or renewing resolution or decision, unless otherwise renewed, adapted, merged, replaced, or retired by a decision of Member States.

### Para. 21 — Criteria for Mandate Decisions
Member States **decide** to develop measurable, clear, and objective criteria to guide decisions on the renewal, adaptation, merger, replacement, or retirement of General Assembly mandates, informed by accurate data and impartial expertise and analysis.

### Para. 22 — Review of Existing Stock
Member States **agree** to undertake a review of the existing stock of mandates from General Assembly resolutions (without duplicating existing review processes), with a view to taking decisions on their renewal, adaptation, merger, replacement, or retirement, and to develop modalities to guide this review.

### Para. 23 — Other Intergovernmental Organs
**Calls upon** other intergovernmental organs across the UN system to review their existing stock of mandates in accordance with their respective intergovernmental decision-making processes.

### Para. 24 — Inactive and Duplicative Mandates
**Requests** the Secretary-General to review mandates to identify those that are inactive, duplicative, or fully implemented, for presentation to Member States **by the end of December 2026**, for their consideration.

### Para. 25 — Excluded Mandates
Member States **agree** that the following shall **not** be subject to the reviews in paragraphs 22, 23, and 24 (unless otherwise agreed by Member States):
- Mandates that are in the Charter
- Norm-setting of universal character
- Institution-creating mandates
- Mandates relating to ongoing political situations with implications for international peace and security

### Para. 26 — Periodicity of Agenda Items
Member States **agree** to review the frequency with which agenda items, resolutions, and decisions are considered, taking a more strategic and streamlined approach, which may include:
- Biennializing annually considered resolutions/decisions
- Triennializing biennially considered resolutions/decisions

## 5. System-Wide Coordination and Coherence

### Para. 27 — Member State Coordination
Member States **agree** to improve their intergovernmental coordination and oversight mechanisms to enhance strategic coordination and coherence across the UN system.

### Para. 28 — Chief Executives Board Review
**Requests** the Secretary-General (in capacity as Chair of the Chief Executives Board) to regularly review mechanisms that coordinate across the UN system to foster a culture of continuous improvement and enhance strategic coordination and coherence.

### Para. 29 — Digital Tools
**Requests** the Secretary-General to provide tools and reference materials to support Member States' decision-making throughout the mandate life cycle, including:
- Further developing practical, interoperable digital tools and portals (in addition to the Mandate Registry)
- Providing consolidated, timely, and comparable information on mandates, resources, activities, and results
- Using common definitions and reporting standards to the extent possible
- Ensuring that any AI-based tools have appropriate safeguards and effective human oversight

### Para. 30 — Ongoing Responsiveness
**Requests** the Secretary-General to remain responsive to Member State requests for enhanced functionality of the Mandate Registry and other digital tools, and to provide ongoing guidance and training.

### Para. 31 — Presiding Officers
**Calls upon** presiding officers and bureaux of intergovernmental organs to improve efficiency and effectiveness of working methods, share best practices, and strive for harmonization through enhanced collaboration.

### Para. 32 — Consistent Approach
**Calls upon** all intergovernmental organs to take a consistent approach to mandate creation, implementation, and review by applying the principles and actions outlined in this resolution.

## 6. Next Steps — Ad Hoc Working Group on Mandate Implementation Review

### Para. 33 — Working Group Mandate and Structure

The General Assembly **decides** to continue the work of the informal ad hoc working group as the **Ad Hoc Working Group on Mandate Implementation Review** on the following basis:

- **(a)** Open to all Member States and observers.
- **(b)** The President of the General Assembly will appoint two Co-Chairs.
- **(c)** The Working Group resumes **by 1 May 2026** and concludes **no later than 30 April 2027**.
- **(d)** Workplan:

| Deadline | Task |
|---|---|
| End of May 2026 | Develop concept note template, resolution/decision templates, model review clauses, and model retirement clauses |
| End of July 2026 | Receive and consider Secretary-General's review of mandated reports (per para. 16) |
| End of August 2026 | Develop criteria for renewal/adaptation/merger/replacement/retirement of GA mandates (per para. 21) |
| End of August 2026 | Develop modalities to guide review of existing stock of GA mandates (per paras. 22 and 25) |
| Mid-December 2026 | Receive Secretary-General's advice on collective/clustered reviews (per para. 18); explore options |
| Mid-February 2027 | Facilitate engagement on Mandate Registry and other tools (per paras. 6, 29, 30) |
| End of March 2027 | Receive and consider Secretary-General's review of inactive, duplicative, or fully implemented mandates (per para. 24) |

- **(e)** Co-Chairs will provide a detailed road map to Working Group members.
- **(f)** Co-Chairs will provide the General Assembly with a progress update by the end of the eightieth session.
- **(g)** Member States will strive for consensus in deliberations.
- **(h)** The Secretary-General will provide substantive support to the Working Group as needed.
